Visit Sonoma

...much to do in the Valley of the Moon

Sonoma State Historic Park

Sonoma State Historic park includes a multiple historic sites including Mission San Francisco Solono, The Sonoma Barracks and General Vallejo's Home. For more information, visit their Website. Photo credit: City of Sonoma

Sonoma Plaza

Sonoma Plaza provides a lush and shady park in the center of town, featuring Sonoma City hall in the center. The blocks surrounding the Plaza offers a wide variety of shops, restuarants and tasting rooms. Sonoma Plaza Website Photo credit: Teri Appleton

Buena Vista Winery

Sonoma's oldest Winery! Founded in 1857 by the “Count of Buena Vista,” Agoston Haraszthy and by 1943 was purchased by Frank and Antonia Bartholomew and given new life. For more information, visit their website Photo credit: sonomacounty.com

Sonoma Overlook Trail

Sonoma Overlook Trail is an easily accessible 3 mile trail near sonoma Plaza. For more information, visit their website Photo Credit: Teri Appleton

Glen Ellen and Kenwood

Head up Highway 12 just north of Sonoma and check out the rest of Sonoma Valley. Glen Ellen and Kenwood are along the way and boasts many wineries and more fantastic dining. Visit Jack London State Park or Sugarloaf Ridge for hiking and a picnic. Photo credit: sonomacounty.com

Traintown

Have kids? A family friendly option is Traintown! A rail themed amusement park with working trains and other fun rides and attractions. For more information, visit their website Photo credit: Traintown
